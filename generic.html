Case2

This case is sample dataset of Food Delivery in Manhattan during 18.00 PM - 23.59 PM.
Case2 emerged as a result of my curiosity regarding how to manage unfamiliar datasets.

Dataset information

The dataset contains valuable information regarding order_id, food category, shop, and sales.
However, it is missing a crucial piece of data - the distance between the shop and the drop-off point.
Fortunately, the dataset does include latitude and longitude columns, which can be leveraged to calculate the distance between the two points.

Distance Calculation in DAX

As per the previous article, I needed to determine the distance between two locations using latitude and longitude.
Power BI does not have a built-in feature for calculating distance based on location points.
Therefore, I used "The Haversin Formula" for calculating the distance between two points on the surface.
Here's the formula. 

  
d = 2r * asin(sqrt(sin^2((lat2-lat1)/2) + cos(lat1) * cos(lat2) * sin^2((lon2-lon1)/2)))
where:		
d = distance between the two points in units of the radius of the sphere (usually kilometers or miles)
r = radius of the sphere (usually the Earth's radius in the desired units)
lat1, lat2 = latitude of point 1 and point 2 in radians
lon1, lon2 = longitude of point 1 and point 2 in radians

Then, Generated the formula into DAX function.

Report information

The report consists of 2 pages. The first page is an Overview page that contains most of the requirements related to sales information. The main focus of this page is the branch performance and sales insights of the company.
The second page is the Customer page which displays the details of the branches/customers, such as the total number of shops in each branch or the total sales of every customer.
